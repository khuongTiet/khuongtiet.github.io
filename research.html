User Research

View the user interviews
U s e r   I n t e r v i e w s
The user interviews really helped me gauge what people expected to see in a portfolio. The consensus was that most people thought of a portfolio as a visual representation of your resume. As such I designed my portfolio to fit this sort of format. I do feel that the interview questions strongly leaned towards an overall grasp rather than personal questions and input. I decided to conduct my interviews across a wide range of people. I interviewed fellow students in the class, a person within the overarching school of Computer Science but not in the same department, as well as a person that is majoring in Fashion. I feel like for any kind of work that involves design, there will be a portfolio so it would be good practice to hear what kinds of inputs these people would have. Portfolios are visual so I feel like the answers that I received from the fashion major really helped me shape out the design for my portfolio.
View the competitor analysis
C o m p e t i t o r   A n a l y s i s
A competitor analysis is a look at the peers that are doing what you want to be doing. It's essentially scoping out the competition to gain insight on what to do. Conducting competitor analyses also assisted heavily in how I wanted to style and format my website. Of course I had an idea of what I wanted but there are just so many ways of laying out a website that I needed inspiration to draw from. I wanted to stray away from the very basic Bootstrap-esque layout with the hero image and three sections with a fixed navigation bar at the top. I found a nice middle ground between that and something new. The biggest inspiration that I had draw from was a film studios' website.
View the feature value matrix
F e a t u r e   V a l u e   M a t r i x
A feature value matrix is a chart of features that you would want to implement along with a scoring system on how valuable it is to you. The features that I picked were ones that you would expect to see on a resume. I wanted to have a navigation bar because I feel like that is something that is expected by now so I had to have it. Having a picture of myself was important as well because I want the person viewing the portfolio to attribute all that they see to a face. It was a bit difficult scoring each feature because I already had in mind what I wanted so the scoring might have been a bit biased towards certain ones. In the future I believe that I would come into this process without having a set of features that I would want to implement before hand and gather the features during the process not before. That is not to say its bad practice to have some features that I would want to see in the end design but for the gathering stage I feel that it would help me if I came into the process with a clean mind and a clean slate with nothing to compare to.
View the feature prioritization
F e a t u r e   P r i o r i t i z a t i o n
I prioritized on the features that I felt like could have been done realistically in the time frame that I had, as well as based upon how much I wanted them. Using the value scores from the feature value matrix helped in deciding which ones to implement but I also had my own judgement in which ones to pick. Prioritizing features really helps you with the way you think about how you want to layout the interface. Having a set of features in mind helps you build out the core of what you think you would want, and then you can add on additional features as you see fit. This is how I approached the rest of my portfolio after really deciding upon core features that I wanted to keep no matter what.

User Testing

View the user stories
U s e r   S t o r i e s
User stories are vital to the creation of any product or design. They're what helps you drive your overall design and really lets you have a look into what you should be producing. By having user stories you are able to forge your design into something that guides users and helps to get what they really need out of your design. The way I arrived at creating my user stories was to have a look at my features, decide what kind of user would be likely to want or use that feature, and then I would go ahead and try to find that feature as if I were that user. I put myself in the user's shoes and set out with a goal in mind to use that feature. This way really helped me visualize what kind of interactions would be happening at each section of my portfolio.
View the cognitive walkthroughs
C o g n i t i v e   W a l k t h r o u g h s
A cognitive walkthough is essentially leading a user through your work, giving them instructions or tasks to do something, and then taking note of how they interact with it and the outcome of the tasks. Cognitive walkthroughs really help with actually seeing how a user interacts with your design first hand and really showcases the strengths and weaknesses of what you have designed. There were some cases in which conducting the cognitive walkthrough was a bit frustrating because I knew how the portfolio worked and I had the design in mind but of course that did not translate well into how other people would see and use the site. It was a very good exercise to conduct the walkthroughs though as they really helped me see the biggest flaws in my design and really assisted in the overall redesign of the portfolio.

U s e r   T e s t s
For the user tests, I had chosen to use a navigation test and a 5 second visual test. I feel like initially I knew that one of the main problems with my previous design was the visuals of it. I felt like the colors were clashing so I wanted to have users try to navigate through the site and let me know what they thought about the overall design and color scheme that I had. The tests really helped me understand where users would initially look for certain sections in my portfolio as well as helping me get the opinions of people on the coloring that I had set up for my sections. It really helped that some of my tests were anonymous as it allowed the test taker to have free reign in terms of how they would critique me as I believe nothing really comes from kind and sweet critique. Challenges that I had come to terms with were how limited I was able to actually test and see the results for the test. I feel that the way I had tested the portfolio was a half hearted cognitive walkthrough and that I would have had more cognitive walkthroughs to base my work off of instead of conducting these kinds of tests. Certainly a different type of test would have proven more useful but I did not go down that route during this process.

Summary of Findings

Through the research conducted, I feel like I have a stronger grasp on what people generally expect to see when they view a portfolio or want to view one. A portfolio is the visual representation of you and all your achievements. The results that stemmed from my research helped me to steer a large portion of my design and layout to fit the expectations set forth from the people that I had conducted my testing and research with. It was understood that the portfolio should be a sort of visual resume but not plain like the actual resume. This was a chance to showcase my abilities and therefore it was imperative to provide a sense of overall theme and design with the portfolio. I feel like this process may be a neverending one as I wish to continue to work on this portfolio and therefore will always need some from of user testing.

I find that I had came into this portfolio with a set design and list of features that I wanted to implement but as I got through the actual process I had learned that what I wanted was essentially different from what I actually needed. I really wanted to use a color theme that revolved around the color purple and shades of it but at the same time I wanted to have a sort of hero image banner at the top of my portfolio. These two clashed due to the nature of my photo so I had to decide upon one or the other. Through user testing I found that people generally preferred the hero image and rather me switch up the color theme. It was through this that I was able to make more progress on the overall portfolio and I valued the findings that I was able to gather.

Incorporation of Findings

Incorporating the findings was much easier than actually going out and doing the research and testing in my opinion. With a list of opinions and Google, I was able to tailor the portfolio to fit the expectations that came with the thought of a portfolio while keeping the design that I had envisioned for it. Of course, there were some difficulties trying to incorporate everything that I had wanted so I am not completely finished with the portfolio. The incorporation of what I gained from the findings did not alter my vision too much which was something that I was really excited about. I was able to improve my portfolio without sacrificing what I wanted it to look like.

Removing the clashing colors was the first step to incoporating the changes that people thought I should make. I decided to have a neutral black color replace the purple but I wanted to not have it be so plain so I utilized the 'rgba' property to add a bit of effect and depth to the main sections. I want to say that this draws more emphasis to what the box contains due to the contrasting colors of a black shade and a white box but that wasn't what I had planned for and it was just what it happened to turn out to be. I found that while incorporating the new changes requested, I was able to discover more about what I want the overall design to look like.
